# Business Model

## 1. Executive Summary

This document defines the business model of **StackLeo Tech Store** — how the company creates value, delivers value, and generates revenue while remaining scalable for future expansion. It serves as the official business model reference for the project and should inform all business, product, and operational decisions.

StackLeo Tech Store operates as a technology and electronics retail business, currently selling directly to consumers (B2C) across an online store and a physical retail store in Bangladesh. The business model is intentionally designed to be future-ready, supporting a planned evolution toward corporate sales, wholesale distribution, and a multi-vendor marketplace, delivered across an expanding set of sales channels.

This document describes the business model at a strategic level. It does not describe implementation approach, technology choices, system architecture, or database design, all of which are addressed in dedicated technical documentation elsewhere in the repository.

## 2. Business Vision

StackLeo Tech Store's business model is guided by the vision defined in `00_Project_Overview/overview.md`: to become the most trusted and preferred technology marketplace in Bangladesh, and a recognized name in the region's technology e-commerce industry.

The business model is structured to support this vision by building a reliable, trust-driven single-seller foundation first, then scaling deliberately toward broader business models and sales channels once that foundation is validated.

## 5. Customer Relationships

StackLeo Tech Store builds customer relationships through:

- Consistent, trustworthy service across both online and physical retail channels.
- Responsive customer support for pre-sale questions and after-sales issues.
- Transparent pricing, product information, and warranty terms.
- Ongoing engagement through future capabilities such as premium membership and loyalty-oriented offerings.
- Dedicated relationship management for future corporate and wholesale customers, reflecting the more consultative nature of business-to-business engagement.

## 12. Business Operations Overview

StackLeo Tech Store's operations currently center on two coordinated sales channels: the online store and the physical retail store. Both channels draw from a shared product catalog and inventory position, supported by common supplier, logistics, and payment partnerships.

Operational consistency between channels is a core business requirement, ensuring customers receive the same pricing, product information, and service standard regardless of how they choose to shop. As the business grows, operations are expected to expand to support additional channels, including a mobile app, marketplace, corporate portal, and wholesale portal, without disrupting the core operating model.

## 14. Growth Strategy

StackLeo Tech Store's growth strategy follows a phased approach:

1. Establish trust and operational reliability as a single-seller B2C business across online and physical retail channels.
2. Expand customer reach through additional channels, including a mobile app.
3. Introduce corporate sales and wholesale capabilities to serve organizational and bulk buyers.
4. Introduce multi-vendor marketplace capabilities to expand product breadth and platform reach.
5. Pursue regional expansion beyond Bangladesh once the primary market is well established.

This phased approach is designed to validate each stage of growth before committing further investment to the next, consistent with the roadmap defined in `00_Project_Overview/project-roadmap.md`.

## 15. Marketplace Expansion Strategy

StackLeo Tech Store's long-term strategy includes evolving from a single-seller platform into a multi-vendor marketplace. This expansion is expected to proceed as follows:

- Validate the single-seller B2C model and establish a strong brand reputation as a prerequisite for marketplace expansion.
- Introduce a curated, verified onboarding process for third-party sellers to preserve product authenticity and customer trust.
- Maintain consistent quality, pricing transparency, and customer service standards across all sellers on the marketplace.
- Introduce marketplace commission as a new revenue stream, supplementing direct product sales.
- Expand product category breadth through third-party sellers, complementing StackLeo's own catalog rather than replacing it.

## 16. Franchise Expansion Strategy

As the physical retail presence proves successful, StackLeo may consider a franchise-based model to expand its physical footprint efficiently:

- Establish and validate a consistent, replicable retail store model before considering franchise expansion.
- Define clear brand, product, and service standards that franchise locations would be required to maintain.
- Prioritize franchise expansion within Bangladesh's key commercial areas before considering broader geographic reach.
- Maintain centralized control over product sourcing, pricing, and brand standards to preserve consistency across franchise locations.

Franchise expansion is a directional, long-term consideration and is not part of the project's current scope, as defined in `00_Project_Overview/project-scope.md`.

## 17. Risk Analysis

| Risk | Potential Impact | Mitigation Direction |
|---|---|---|
| Inconsistent experience across channels | Erosion of customer trust in the brand. | Maintain unified catalog, pricing, and service standards across channels. |
| Slower-than-expected adoption of new channels | Delayed return on investment for expansion initiatives. | Phase channel expansion based on validated demand. |
| Dependence on key suppliers or distributors | Disruption to product availability. | Diversify supplier relationships where feasible. |
| Increased competitive pressure | Reduced market share or pricing pressure. | Reinforce trust-based differentiation and service quality. |
| Marketplace seller quality issues (future) | Damage to brand trust from third-party seller performance. | Apply a verified, curated seller onboarding process. |

A complete assessment of business risks is documented in `business-requirements.md` and `swot-analysis.md`.
