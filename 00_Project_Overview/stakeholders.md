# Project Stakeholders

## 1. Document Purpose

This document identifies the stakeholders involved in or affected by the **StackLeo Tech Store** project. It defines their roles, responsibilities, interests, expectations, and influence on the project's direction and success.

This document provides a clear understanding of who participates in the project and how each stakeholder contributes to, or is affected by, its outcomes. It is intended to support communication, accountability, and decision-making across all phases of the project.

This document does not describe implementation details, technical architecture, or system design. Stakeholder involvement in technical decisions, where applicable, is addressed through dedicated technical documentation elsewhere in the repository.

## 2. Stakeholder Overview

Stakeholders in the StackLeo Tech Store project are grouped into two categories:

- **Internal Stakeholders** — individuals and teams within StackLeo who are directly responsible for planning, building, and operating the project.
- **External Stakeholders** — individuals, groups, and organizations outside StackLeo who influence, depend on, or are affected by the project.

Both categories play an essential role in the project's success. Internal stakeholders drive execution and decision-making, while external stakeholders shape requirements, provide critical services, and ultimately determine the platform's market success.

## 3. Internal Stakeholders

| Stakeholder | Description |
|---|---|
| Founder / Business Owner | Sets the overall business direction, vision, and strategic priorities for StackLeo and StackLeo Tech Store. |
| Project Lead / Product Manager | Owns the project's planning, scope, goals, and roadmap, and coordinates across all contributing teams. |
| Business Analyst | Documents requirements, scope, and stakeholder needs, and ensures alignment between business goals and project execution. |
| Solution Architect / Technical Lead | Defines the high-level technical direction of the platform and ensures it supports the project's business and product goals. |
| Development Team | Responsible for building the platform's backend, frontend, and administrative capabilities as the project progresses. |
| Quality Assurance Team | Validates that the platform meets defined functional, quality, and reliability expectations before release. |
| Operations Team | Manages day-to-day business operations, including order fulfillment, customer support, and inventory coordination. |
| Marketing & Growth Team | Builds brand awareness, drives customer acquisition, and communicates the platform's value proposition to the market. |

## 4. External Stakeholders

| Stakeholder | Description |
|---|---|
| Customers | Individual consumers in Bangladesh who purchase technology and electronics products through the platform. |
| Product Suppliers / Vendors | Parties that supply StackLeo with the technology and electronics products offered on the platform. |
| Delivery & Logistics Partners | Third parties responsible for transporting and delivering ordered products to customers. |
| Payment Service Providers | Third parties that enable and process customer payment transactions on the platform. |
| Regulatory & Government Authorities | Bodies responsible for laws and regulations governing e-commerce, consumer protection, and business operations in Bangladesh. |
| Future Business Customers (B2B) | Organizations expected to engage with the platform once B2B capabilities are introduced. |
| Future Marketplace Sellers | Third-party vendors expected to join the platform once multi-vendor marketplace capabilities are introduced. |

## 7. Stakeholder Expectations

- **Founder / Business Owner** expects the project to deliver a credible, trustworthy, and sustainable business outcome aligned with StackLeo's long-term vision.
- **Project Lead / Product Manager** expects clear scope, goals, and roadmap alignment to guide consistent execution across all teams.
- **Business Analyst** expects accurate, complete, and current documentation that reflects the project's true direction.
- **Solution Architect / Technical Lead** expects business goals and scope to be well-defined before technical direction is finalized.
- **Development, Quality Assurance, and Operations Teams** expect clear requirements and realistic timelines to deliver a reliable platform.
- **Marketing & Growth Team** expects a product and brand positioning that can be effectively communicated to the target market.
- **Customers** expect a simple, trustworthy, and reliable marketplace experience consistent with the platform's tagline and mission.
- **Suppliers and Logistics Partners** expect clear, dependable business arrangements and manageable operational expectations.
- **Payment Service Providers** expect compliant, well-integrated, and predictable transaction processes.
- **Regulatory Authorities** expect the business to operate within applicable laws and consumer protection standards.
- **Future B2B Customers and Marketplace Sellers** expect the platform to eventually support their respective business needs, consistent with the future goals defined in `project-goals.md`.

## 9. Stakeholder Engagement Strategy

- Engage internal stakeholders continuously throughout planning, development, and operational phases to maintain alignment with project scope and goals.
- Engage customers primarily through the platform experience itself, supported by responsive customer support and clear communication channels.
- Maintain reliable, transparent working relationships with suppliers, logistics partners, and payment service providers to ensure consistent operational performance.
- Monitor applicable regulatory requirements proactively to maintain compliance as the business and platform grow.
- Defer active engagement with future B2B customers and marketplace sellers until the corresponding capabilities are prioritized in `project-roadmap.md`.
- Reassess stakeholder priorities at the conclusion of each major project phase to ensure engagement effort remains proportionate to each stakeholder's current relevance.

## 10. Stakeholder Review Process

Stakeholder information is expected to evolve as the project progresses from planning through MVP launch and future expansion. The following principles guide this process:

- Stakeholder roles and priorities should be reviewed at the conclusion of each major phase defined in `project-roadmap.md`.
- Any addition, removal, or change to a stakeholder's role, responsibility, or priority must be reflected by updating this document and incrementing its version number.
- All changes to stakeholder information must be recorded in `changelog.md`, including the reasoning behind the change.
- Stakeholder reviews should confirm continued alignment with `project-scope.md` and `project-goals.md`.
- New stakeholder categories, such as future B2B customers or marketplace sellers, should be formally incorporated into this document once the corresponding capabilities become active project priorities.
