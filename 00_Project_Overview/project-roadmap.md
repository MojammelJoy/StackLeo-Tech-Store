# Project Roadmap

## 1. Document Purpose

This document defines the high-level development roadmap for the **StackLeo Tech Store** project. It presents the major phases of the project, from initial planning through production launch and future expansion, and describes the intent and expected outcome of each phase.

This document provides strategic direction and sequencing. It does not commit to exact calendar dates, and it does not describe implementation details, technology choices, database structure, or API design, all of which are addressed in dedicated documentation elsewhere in the repository.

Timelines and delivery dates, where required for internal planning purposes, are managed separately from this document and are not part of its scope.

## 2. Roadmap Overview

The StackLeo Tech Store roadmap is organized into sequential phases, progressing from foundational planning to a live, operating marketplace, followed by ongoing optimization and future expansion.

| Phase | Focus |
|---|---|
| Phase 1 | Project Planning & Documentation |
| Phase 2 | System Design |
| Phase 3 | Database Design |
| Phase 4 | Backend Development |
| Phase 5 | Frontend Development |
| Phase 6 | Admin Dashboard |
| Phase 7 | Testing & Quality Assurance |
| Phase 8 | Deployment & Launch |
| Phase 9 | Post-Launch Optimization |

Each phase builds upon the outcomes of the phase before it. While phases are presented sequentially, some may overlap in practice as the project progresses, provided that the foundational outcomes of earlier phases have been achieved.

## 3. Phase 1 — Project Planning & Documentation

**Objective:** Establish a clear, shared foundation for the project before development begins.

This phase focuses on defining the project's purpose, scope, goals, stakeholders, and governance structures. It ensures that all teams and contributors share a consistent understanding of what StackLeo Tech Store is, what it aims to achieve, and what boundaries apply, before any design or development work starts.

**Key Outcomes:**

- Foundational documentation established, including project overview, scope, and goals.
- Stakeholders identified and their roles clarified.
- Assumptions and constraints recorded.
- Shared terminology defined to support consistent communication across teams.

This is the current phase of the project, reflected in its **Planning & Documentation** status.

## 4. Phase 2 — System Design

**Objective:** Translate the project's business and product goals into a coherent, high-level system design.

This phase focuses on defining how the platform's major functional areas fit together to support the goals defined in `project-goals.md`, establishing a shared design direction for subsequent development phases.

**Key Outcomes:**

- High-level functional and structural design of the platform defined.
- Design decisions validated against the project's scope and goals.
- A shared design reference established to guide backend, frontend, and admin dashboard development.

## 5. Phase 3 — Database Design

**Objective:** Define the structure required to reliably support the platform's data and business processes.

This phase focuses on planning how information relevant to the marketplace will be organized and maintained, ensuring the foundation is reliable, consistent, and able to support the platform's current and future needs.

**Key Outcomes:**

- Data structure planning completed to support core marketplace functionality.
- Data design validated against the platform's functional requirements.
- A stable foundation established for backend development.

## 6. Phase 4 — Backend Development

**Objective:** Build the core systems that power the marketplace's functionality.

This phase focuses on developing the underlying capabilities required to support product discovery, purchasing, order handling, and other core marketplace operations, in alignment with the system and data design established in earlier phases.

**Key Outcomes:**

- Core marketplace capabilities developed and functional.
- Backend systems aligned with the platform's scope and design direction.
- A stable foundation established to support frontend and admin dashboard development.

## 7. Phase 5 — Frontend Development

**Objective:** Build the customer-facing experience of the marketplace.

This phase focuses on developing the interface through which customers discover, evaluate, and purchase products, ensuring the experience reflects the customer experience goals defined in `project-goals.md`.

**Key Outcomes:**

- Customer-facing marketplace experience developed.
- Interface aligned with the platform's brand and customer experience goals.
- Core customer journeys, from discovery to order completion, functional and cohesive.

## 8. Phase 6 — Admin Dashboard

**Objective:** Provide the internal tools required to manage and operate the marketplace.

This phase focuses on developing the internal capabilities StackLeo requires to manage products, orders, and platform operations, supporting the operational goals defined in `project-goals.md`.

**Key Outcomes:**

- Internal tools developed to support day-to-day marketplace operations.
- Administrative capabilities aligned with operational and business needs.
- A foundation established for reliable, ongoing platform management.

## 9. Phase 7 — Testing & Quality Assurance

**Objective:** Validate that the platform meets the project's quality, reliability, and experience expectations.

This phase focuses on verifying that the marketplace functions correctly and consistently across customer-facing and administrative capabilities, prior to launch.

**Key Outcomes:**

- Core marketplace functionality validated for correctness and reliability.
- Issues identified and addressed prior to production deployment.
- Platform readiness confirmed against the goals and scope defined earlier in the project.

## 10. Phase 8 — Deployment & Launch

**Objective:** Bring StackLeo Tech Store into live operation for customers in Bangladesh.

This phase focuses on preparing and executing the transition from a completed, validated platform to a live, customer-facing marketplace, marking the delivery of the MVP defined in `project-scope.md`.

**Key Outcomes:**

- Platform deployed and made available to customers.
- Launch readiness confirmed across business and operational functions.
- MVP scope delivered and operational in the primary market.

## 11. Phase 9 — Post-Launch Optimization

**Objective:** Refine and strengthen the platform based on real-world usage and feedback.

This phase focuses on observing how the platform performs and how customers engage with it following launch, and using those insights to improve reliability, usability, and overall customer trust.

**Key Outcomes:**

- Customer feedback and platform performance reviewed on an ongoing basis.
- Improvements identified and prioritized based on real-world usage.
- Platform stability and customer experience strengthened ahead of future expansion.

## 12. Future Roadmap

Following the successful completion of the MVP and post-launch optimization, the project's future direction is expected to focus on the following areas, consistent with the long-term goals defined in `project-goals.md`:

- Introduction of multi-vendor marketplace capabilities.
- Introduction of business-to-business (B2B) sales capabilities.
- Expansion of product categories beyond the initial MVP catalog.
- Regional market expansion beyond Bangladesh.

The sequencing, prioritization, and detailed planning of these future phases will be defined at the appropriate time, based on the outcomes and learnings of the earlier phases.

## 13. Roadmap Review Process

The roadmap is expected to evolve as the project progresses and as new information becomes available. The following principles guide how the roadmap is maintained:

- The roadmap should be reviewed at the conclusion of each major phase to confirm continued alignment with the project's scope and goals.
- Any change to the phases, sequencing, or direction described in this document must be reflected by updating this document and incrementing its version number.
- All roadmap changes must be recorded in `changelog.md`, including the reasoning behind the change.
- Roadmap reviews should confirm continued alignment with `project-scope.md` and `project-goals.md`, and should account for any updates to `assumptions.md` or `constraints.md`.
