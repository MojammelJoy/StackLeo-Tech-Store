# Project Scope

## 1. Scope Overview

This document defines the complete scope of the **StackLeo Tech Store** project. It establishes what is included in the project, what is explicitly excluded, and how the scope is expected to evolve over time through a defined MVP (Minimum Viable Product) stage and subsequent future enhancements.

This document serves as the official scope reference for all current and future development. Any work that falls outside the boundaries defined here should not be assumed to be part of the project unless this document, or its successor version, is formally updated.

This document does not describe how the project will be built. Implementation details, technical architecture, API design, database structure, and technology choices are intentionally excluded and are documented separately elsewhere in the repository.

## 2. Project Boundaries

The scope of StackLeo Tech Store is defined at three levels:

- **Business Boundaries** — the market, customer base, and business model the project serves.
- **Functional Boundaries** — the categories of capability the platform is expected to provide to customers and the business.
- **Phase Boundaries** — the distinction between what is delivered in the MVP and what is planned for later stages.

Anything not explicitly identified as in scope, in a current or future phase, should be considered out of scope until formally added through the scope change process described in this document.

## 3. In Scope

The following areas are within the scope of the StackLeo Tech Store project:

- A single, centralized online marketplace for technology and electronics products.
- Direct-to-consumer (B2C) retail operations serving customers in Bangladesh.
- Organized product categories covering computers, mobile devices, audio and wearables, networking and smart home devices, gaming and entertainment devices, and general electronics and accessories.
- A customer-facing shopping experience that supports product discovery, purchasing, and order fulfillment.
- Foundational business documentation, planning, and governance required to guide the project's development.
- A platform structure designed to support future expansion into B2B sales and multi-vendor marketplace operations.

## 4. Out of Scope

The following areas are explicitly excluded from the current project scope:

- Operating as a multi-vendor marketplace where third-party sellers list and manage their own products.
- Business-to-business (B2B) sales processes, bulk ordering, or organizational account management.
- Physical retail operations, including brick-and-mortar stores or in-person sales channels.
- Expansion into markets outside Bangladesh.
- Manufacturing, importing, or physical warehousing operations owned or operated by StackLeo.
- Any product category outside technology and electronics.
- Detailed technical architecture, API design, database schema, and technology stack decisions, which are governed by separate technical documentation.

## 5. MVP Scope

The Minimum Viable Product (MVP) represents the first release of StackLeo Tech Store and focuses on validating the core marketplace concept with the smallest complete offering that delivers value to customers.

The MVP is scoped to include:

- A single-seller (StackLeo-operated) online marketplace for technology and electronics products.
- Core product categories necessary to demonstrate a credible, tech-focused catalog.
- A straightforward, trustworthy customer shopping experience from product discovery through to order completion.
- Business-to-consumer (B2C) operations only.
- Foundational documentation, planning, and governance required to support a stable initial launch.

The MVP is intentionally limited in scope to allow the business to validate demand, trust, and operational readiness before pursuing broader capabilities.

## 6. Future Enhancements

The following capabilities are recognized as part of the project's long-term direction but are not part of the MVP. They will be considered for future phases once the MVP has been delivered and validated:

- Introduction of multi-vendor marketplace capabilities, allowing third-party sellers to join the platform.
- Introduction of business-to-business (B2B) sales capabilities for organizational and bulk customers.
- Expansion of product categories beyond the initial MVP catalog.
- Regional market expansion beyond Bangladesh.
- Additional customer engagement and loyalty capabilities.

Inclusion in this section indicates future intent only. Prioritization, timelines, and detailed planning for these enhancements will be addressed in `project-roadmap.md`.

## 7. Business Scope

StackLeo Tech Store operates under a **Business-to-Consumer (B2C)** business model, with StackLeo as the sole seller on the platform. The business scope is intentionally kept future-ready, allowing the model to evolve toward B2B sales and multi-vendor marketplace operations without requiring a redefinition of the company's overall business direction.

The primary market for the business scope is Bangladesh. Regional or international market expansion is not part of the current business scope and will be evaluated as a future strategic decision.

## 8. Technical Scope (High-Level Only)

At a high level, the project scope includes the delivery of a customer-facing online marketplace platform capable of supporting product discovery and purchasing for a single-seller, B2C business model, with the ability to scale toward additional business models over time.

This document does not define technology choices, system architecture, integrations, API design, or database structure. These details are governed by dedicated technical documentation maintained elsewhere in the repository and are outside the purpose of this document.

## 9. Success Criteria

The scope defined in this document is considered successfully delivered when:

- The MVP is launched and operational as a single-seller, B2C marketplace serving customers in Bangladesh.
- Customers are able to reliably discover and purchase products across the defined MVP product categories.
- The platform demonstrates the trust and reliability described in the project's vision and mission.
- The foundational documentation and governance structures needed to support future growth are in place and actively maintained.

Detailed, measurable objectives and outcomes are defined separately in `project-goals.md`.

## 10. Scope Change Policy

Scope is expected to evolve as the project matures, particularly as it transitions from MVP toward future enhancements. To maintain clarity and prevent uncontrolled scope growth, the following policy applies:

- Any proposed addition, removal, or modification to the defined scope must be reviewed against this document before being accepted.
- Approved scope changes must be reflected by updating this document and incrementing its version number.
- All scope changes must be recorded in `changelog.md`, including the nature of the change and the reasoning behind it.
- Work that has not been formally added to this document's in-scope sections should not be treated as approved project scope.
- Significant scope changes should be reviewed against `assumptions.md` and `constraints.md` to confirm continued alignment with the project's foundational planning.
