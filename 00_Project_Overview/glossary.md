# Glossary

## 1. Document Purpose

This document provides a centralized glossary of business, e-commerce, logistics, inventory, payment, customer service, and software terminology used throughout the **StackLeo Tech Store** project documentation.

This glossary helps developers, designers, QA engineers, project managers, stakeholders, and future contributors interpret project terminology consistently, reducing ambiguity across all documentation and communication.

This document does not describe implementation details, technical architecture, API design, or database structure. Where a term relates to a technical concept, only a high-level, business-oriented definition is provided here.

## 2. How to Use This Glossary

- Terms are grouped by subject area to make related terminology easier to locate.
- Within each section, terms are arranged alphabetically.
- If a term used in project documentation is not yet defined here, it should be added at the earliest opportunity to keep this glossary complete.
- Where a term has a specific meaning within the StackLeo Tech Store project that differs from its general industry usage, the project-specific meaning applies.
- This glossary should be treated as the single source of truth for terminology across all documents in this repository.

## 3. Business Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| B2B (Business-to-Business) | A business model in which one business sells products or services to another business, rather than to an individual consumer. |
| B2C (Business-to-Consumer) | A business model in which a business sells products or services directly to individual consumers. StackLeo Tech Store currently operates on this model. |
| Brand Positioning | The way a company presents itself in the market to distinguish itself from competitors, such as StackLeo's focus on trust and reliability. |
| Marketplace | A platform that brings together products, and potentially multiple sellers, for customers to browse and purchase from in one place. |
| MVP (Minimum Viable Product) | The first version of a product, containing only the core features necessary to deliver value and validate the business concept. |
| Primary Market | The main geographic market a business focuses its operations and growth strategy on. For StackLeo Tech Store, this is Bangladesh. |
| Stakeholder | Any individual, group, or organization with an interest in, or influence over, the project's outcomes. |
| Target Audience | The group of customers a product or service is designed to serve. |
| Vision | The long-term aspiration a company or project is working toward. |

## 4. E-Commerce Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Cart (Shopping Cart) | A temporary collection of products a customer intends to purchase before completing an order. |
| Catalog | The complete collection of products available for customers to browse and purchase on the platform. |
| Checkout | The process by which a customer confirms their order details and completes a purchase. |
| Conversion | The point at which a visitor to the platform completes a desired action, such as making a purchase. |
| Customer Journey | The complete experience a customer goes through, from discovering the platform to completing a purchase and beyond. |
| Multi-Vendor Marketplace | An e-commerce model in which multiple independent sellers list and sell products through a single shared platform. |
| Product Listing | The presentation of a single product on the platform, including its details, pricing, and availability. |
| Wishlist | A feature allowing customers to save products they are interested in for future consideration. |

## 5. Product & Inventory Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Category | A grouping used to organize products by type, such as computers, mobile phones, or audio devices. |
| In Stock | The status indicating a product is currently available for purchase. |
| Inventory | The total quantity of products a business holds and makes available for sale. |
| Out of Stock | The status indicating a product is currently unavailable for purchase due to insufficient inventory. |
| Product | Any individual technology or electronics item offered for sale on the platform. |
| Product Variant | A specific version of a product that differs by attributes such as color, size, or storage capacity. |
| SKU (Stock Keeping Unit) | A unique identifier assigned to a distinct product or product variant to track it within inventory. |
| Supplier / Vendor | A party that provides products to StackLeo for sale on the platform. |

## 6. Order Management Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Order | A confirmed request by a customer to purchase one or more products. |
| Order Cancellation | The process of stopping an order from being fulfilled, typically at the request of the customer or business. |
| Order Confirmation | Communication sent to a customer verifying that their order has been successfully placed. |
| Order Fulfillment | The process of preparing, packaging, and dispatching a customer's order for delivery. |
| Order Status | An indicator showing the current stage of an order, such as placed, processing, shipped, or delivered. |
| Refund | The return of payment to a customer, typically following a cancellation or return. |
| Return | The process by which a customer sends a purchased product back to the business, typically for a refund or exchange. |

## 7. Payment Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Cash on Delivery (COD) | A payment method in which the customer pays for their order at the time of delivery, rather than online in advance. |
| Digital Payment | Any payment made electronically, such as through a card, mobile wallet, or online banking. |
| Payment Gateway | A service that enables the secure processing of customer payments on behalf of a business. |
| Payment Method | The specific means by which a customer chooses to pay for an order, such as card payment or cash on delivery. |
| Transaction | A single completed exchange of payment for products between a customer and the business. |

## 8. Shipping & Logistics Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Delivery | The process of transporting an order from the business to the customer's specified location. |
| Delivery Partner | A third-party service responsible for transporting and delivering orders to customers. |
| Estimated Delivery Time | The approximate timeframe within which a customer can expect to receive their order. |
| Logistics | The overall coordination of processes involved in moving products from the business to the customer. |
| Shipping Address | The location specified by a customer where their order should be delivered. |
| Tracking | The ability for a customer to monitor the progress of their order from dispatch to delivery. |

## 9. Customer Service Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Customer Feedback | Input provided by customers regarding their experience with the platform, products, or service. |
| Customer Retention | The ability of a business to keep customers returning for repeat purchases over time. |
| Customer Satisfaction | A measure of how well a business's products and service meet customer expectations. |
| Customer Support | The assistance provided to customers before, during, or after a purchase to resolve questions or issues. |
| Service Level | The standard of responsiveness and quality a business commits to in supporting its customers. |

## 10. Software & Technical Terms (High-Level Only)

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Admin Dashboard | An internal tool used by the business to manage products, orders, and platform operations. |
| Backend | The internal, non-customer-facing part of a platform that powers its core functionality. |
| Frontend | The customer-facing part of a platform that users directly see and interact with. |
| Platform | The complete online system, including customer-facing and internal components, that makes up StackLeo Tech Store. |
| Roadmap | A high-level plan outlining the major phases and direction of a project over time. |
| Scope | The defined boundaries of what is included and excluded from a project. |
| System | The overall combination of processes and components that work together to deliver the platform's functionality. |
